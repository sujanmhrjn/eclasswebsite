Introduction

School education is one of the major aspect of a child development. As a student, a child performs various activities, from learning several subjects to gaining knowledge and skills related to them. In addition to the information from the books and theories, a child masters the skills and habits of societal interaction, thereby exploring one’s own potential utilizing it in real-life scenarios. To be precise, a child learns from the books and beyond it.

As a child, one learns lessons, understands art and even learns language, words and expression. It helps them to grow and develop as a human being as a result of the daily interactions with the society and the nature around. There are various activities, especially the Co-Curricular activities, and they are very importance to accomplish the complete development of a child to prepare them and make-ready to face the challenges in an ever-changing world.

A key factor, every academic institution from pre-school to the universities, emphasize the significance of learning beyond the books, despite this, CCAs are not prioritized as much. In our context, there are discussions but there are rarely any event that can bring all the students together to showcase their talents, be it within the book or beyond it. Thus, National Student Championship- Battle of the Braind & Books came into being, an event that can bring students all over the country together so they can showcase what they have learnt in the class and beyond!

THE PARTICIPATION FRAMEWORK

Students of a certain school compete with their classmates on daily quiz and weekly held Art, handwriting and spelling contests. On the basis of daily and weekly winners, the school shall nominate students to participate in the higher level and the winners of those higher levels shall progress to the other levels and finally to the Midas National Student Championship.

All the inter-school events shall be broadcast live on either social media channels or national television. The schools and students shall be recognized with trophies, medals and certificates in each level.

All the cost of participation shall be borne by Midas eClass and there shall be no expense from the school or student for participation.

Event Timeline

These events begin with the beginning of the academic year and will culminate towards the end of the academic session. The physical edition of the event shall commence after Dashain/Tihar vacation.

Mentoring & Judging

Currently, the CCA experts at Midas eClass are training CCA coordinators of various schools on how to effectively blend CCA with the curriculum as well as how to encourage students to participate on such events. The CCA coordinators will work with the respective school authorities to organize the intra-school competitions with support from Midas eClass Team.

There is a set of an independent panel of judges who will evaluate the students’ submissions and identify the winners.

Responsibilities of the School

  • Allocate a contact person/teacher/CCA Coordinator who can announce the names of the winners
  • Act as a bridge between school, Midas and parents.
